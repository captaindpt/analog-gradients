# IC Taiwan Grand Challenge - Program Guidelines

**Organizer:** National Science and Technology Council
**Co-organizers:** Industrial Technology Research Institute, Taipei Computer Association, SEMI Taiwan
**Updated:** 2025/12/1

---

## I. Introduction

The Taiwan Chip-based Industrial Innovation Program (Taiwan CbI) is a collaborative effort by various Taiwanese government ministries. It aims to leverage Taiwan's expertise in semiconductor technology by combining chips with innovations like generative AI.

**Key Goals:**
- Attract global startups and investment
- Use generative AI and chips for industry-wide innovation
- Attract global talent
- Advance heterogeneous integration for overall industry growth

In 2024, the IC Taiwan Grand Challenge debuted to discover promising teams early and connect them with Taiwan, attracting IC-related startups and academic research talents worldwide. **Top selections are invited to establish bases in Taiwan**, linking them with funding to advance chip innovations.

## III. Eligibility

### Who Can Apply
- Startups (established within 8 years)
- Juristic persons
- Academic research institutions
- Individuals

### Requirements
1. Plan to collaborate with Taiwan's semiconductor chip design and manufacturing industry
2. Must own IP rights of the product/technology or have legal licensing
3. Must provide concept paper (initial review) and presentation (secondary review)
4. Submit a **3-minute pitch video** with supporting materials

### Restrictions
- **NOT eligible:** Entities from China (including Hong Kong, Macau) or companies with >30% Chinese ownership
- Previous winners cannot win again (unless different project leader)

## IV. Competition Process

### Round 4 Timeline
- **Opens:** December 19, 2025
- **Closes:** March 19, 2026 (grace period: March 20, 2026)
- **Website:** [ictaiwanchallenge.org](https://ictaiwanchallenge.org)

### Steps
1. **Online Registration** - Fill form and upload documents
2. **Qualification Review** - Organizer confirms eligibility and category
3. **Initial Review** - Concept paper evaluated
4. **Secondary Review** - 20-minute online presentation + Q&A (in English)
5. **Result Announcement** - Published 2 weeks after review completion

## V. Judging Criteria

| Criteria | Weight | Details |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Local Connections** | 40% | Resource requirements in Taiwan, development plans for Taiwan's industries |
| **Value Creation** | 40% | Technology innovation potential, industrial upgrading, economic value |
| **Technological Innovations** | 20% | Emerging applications, new materials, cross-domain integration |

## VI. Awards

### Prize Package
- **USD $30,000** landing prize per winning team
- Access to Taiwan's semiconductor supply chain:
  - EDA tools (Keysight, Synopsys, Cadence)
  - IP licensing (ARM, GUC, M31, Andes, eMemory)
  - IC design services (GUC, CMSC, Brocere, VIA NEXT, Faraday)
  - Shuttle services (TSMC, UMC, PSMC)
  - Testing (Keysight, ITRI)

### Additional Benefits
- **Mentorship** by semiconductor industry experts
- **TTA facilities** in Taipei and Kaohsiung
- **Dedicated mentorship manager** for resource connections
- **Platform** for prototyping to production

### Obligations
- At least one team member must participate in **industry matchmaking in Taiwan for minimum 1 month**
- Must attend **COMPUTEX/InnoVEX** (June 2-5, 2026) for award ceremony and exhibition
